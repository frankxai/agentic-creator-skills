# Content Repurposing Templates

Use these templates to convert one core piece into channel-optimized content.

## Blog Post → LinkedIn Post

**Source**: Full blog post
**Extract**: The single most surprising or actionable insight

```
[Bold opening claim from blog insight]

[2-3 sentences of context — why this matters]

[3-5 bullet points with supporting evidence or sub-insights]

[Closing question or challenge to reader]

Full breakdown: [link to blog post]
```

**Tips:**
- Don't summarize the post — give them the best part and make them want more
- The bullet points can be the blog's subheadings rephrased as takeaways
- The link comes last, not first

---

## Blog Post → Email

**Subject**: Use the blog headline but personalize it with "you" framing
**Preview**: Pose the problem the blog solves

```
Subject: Why your [X] isn't working (and what to do instead)
Preview: Most people do this wrong — here's the fix.

[Personal opener — why you're sharing this today]

Here's what I found works:

[3 key takeaways from the post, condensed to 1-2 sentences each]

[CTA: Read the full breakdown →]

[P.S. Optional — extra context, upcoming related content]
```

## Blog Post → Twitter Thread

```
1/ [Bold hook — the most surprising claim from the post. 1-2 sentences max.]

2/ Most people think [common misconception]. But [contrarian truth].

3/ Here's why: [First key supporting point]

4/ [Second key supporting point]

5/ [Third key supporting point — ideally the most actionable]

6/ [Practical application — how to use this insight]

7/ The full breakdown: [link]
   If you found this useful, follow for more on [topic].
```

## Long Post → Short Quote Cards

Extract 3-5 standalone quotes for visual content:
- Must be self-contained (no "as I mentioned")
- 10-25 words ideal for readability on image
- One clear idea per quote
- Attribute with creator name and optional context

**Template**: `"[Quote]" — [Creator Name]`
